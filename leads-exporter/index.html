Leads Exporter for Google Maps

Export business contacts from Google Maps to CSV or Excel in one click. Names, phones, emails, addresses — ready for outreach in 30 seconds.

Install for Chrome Install for Edge

Leads Exporter for Google Maps is a free Chrome and Edge extension that lets you export business contact information directly from Google Maps search results. Search for any business type and location on Google Maps, click "Export All Leads," and download a clean CSV file with business names, addresses, phone numbers, websites, ratings, review counts, and categories — in under 30 seconds.

The free plan includes 50 leads per day with 7 data fields and CSV export. Pro ($4.9/month) unlocks unlimited exports, automatic email extraction from business websites, contact page URLs, social media links (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter), and Excel export. All exported data is cleaned and standardized — phone numbers are formatted consistently, addresses are split into street, city, state, and ZIP, and column headers follow a standard naming convention ready for CRM import.

Unlike most Google Maps lead tools that require sideloading via developer mode, Leads Exporter is officially listed on the Chrome Web Store and Microsoft Edge Add-ons, so you can install it in one click. The free tier offers 50 leads/day — over 3x more than competing tools that cap at 15 per session.

Features

One-Click Bulk Export

Click "Export All Leads" and the extension auto-scrolls through all Google Maps search results, collecting up to ~120 businesses per search. No manual clicking required.

7 Data Fields (Free)

Business name, full address, phone number, website URL, Google rating, review count, and business category — all included in the free plan.

Email Finder (Pro)

Automatically visits business websites to find publicly available email addresses. For businesses without emails, provides a direct link to their contact page.

Clean, CRM-Ready Data

Phone numbers are formatted consistently, addresses are split into components, and column headers are standardized. Export directly to your CRM with no cleanup needed.

Data Preview Before Download

Preview your leads in a built-in table before exporting. Check data quality, filter by name, and select which leads to include in your file.

CSV & Excel Export

Download as CSV (free) or Excel .xlsx (Pro). Both formats are compatible with Google Sheets, Microsoft Excel, HubSpot, Salesforce, and other CRMs.

How It Works

1

Search on Google Maps

Open Google Maps and search for any business type + location. For example: "dentist in Los Angeles" or "plumber in Chicago."

2

Click "Export All Leads"

A green button appears above your search results. Click it and the extension auto-scrolls to load all businesses, then collects their contact data.

3

Preview and download

Click the extension icon to see your data in a table. Review it, then click "Download CSV" to save your leads file. Done in 30 seconds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Leads Exporter for Google Maps free?

Yes. The free plan lets you export up to 50 business leads per day from Google Maps, including names, addresses, phone numbers, websites, ratings, review counts, and categories. No sign-up or credit card required.

What data can I export from Google Maps?

Free users get 7 fields: business name, address, phone number, website URL, rating, review count, and category. Pro users additionally get email addresses, contact page URLs, and social media links (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter).

How does the email extraction work?

Pro visits each business website listed on Google Maps and finds publicly available email addresses. Success rate is typically 40-60%, depending on whether the business publishes an email on their site. For businesses without a public email, you get a link to their contact page instead.

Is it safe to use? Will my Google account get banned?

Leads Exporter reads publicly visible information from Google Maps search results. It does not log into your Google account or access private data. The extension uses randomized scrolling intervals to load results naturally and is officially listed on the Chrome Web Store.

Can I import the data into my CRM?

Yes. The exported CSV and Excel files use standardized column headers (Name, Address, Phone, Website, Email, etc.) that are compatible with HubSpot, Salesforce, Zoho, Pipedrive, and most other CRMs.
